History

South American football's continental dream

The Copa Libertadores de América was born in 1960 as South America's answer to the European Cup. The name honors the libertadores, the great liberators of the continent like Bolívar and San Martín. Initially reserved for national champions, it expanded over the decades to include 47 clubs from 10 countries.

The early years were dominated by Uruguay and Argentina: Peñarol and Nacional on one side, Independiente and Estudiantes on the other. The 1980s saw the rise of Brazilian football with Flamengo and Grêmio. In the 1990s and 2000s Boca Juniors became the most successful club of the modern era, while River Plate dominated the 2010s.

In the contemporary era Brazil has emerged as the strongest nation: between 2019 and 2023, four out of five finals were won by Brazilian clubs, with Flamengo, Palmeiras and Fluminense doing most of the winning.

Format

How the tournament works

The tournament starts with qualifying phases followed by a group stage. The top two from each group advance to the Round of 16. From there it is knockout with home and away legs all the way to the final, also a two-legged affair until 2018. From 2019 the final is a single match at a neutral venue.

In Decempionz the format follows the historical structure: a group stage then knockout rounds through to the grand final. For European clubs try UCL Legends; for national teams, the World Cup.

Trivia

Numbers and historical records

🥇

Independiente are the most successful club with 7 titles, earning the nickname "Rey de Copas" (King of Cups).

⚽

Pelé's Santos won the Libertadores in 1962 and 1963. Pelé scored 54 goals in 82 appearances in the competition.

🔥

The 2018 final between Boca Juniors and River Plate — el Superclásico — is the most anticipated in Copa history.

🌎

Ten countries have produced at least one winner. Argentina (26) and Brazil (23) dominate, with Uruguay (8) in third.

🐓

Flamengo 2019 — the epic comeback in the final against River Plate: 0-1 at the 89th minute, then two Gabriel Barbosa goals in the final 4 minutes.

🏙️

Buenos Aires and São Paulo are the cities with the most winning clubs. No European capital can claim a comparable footballing rivalry.
