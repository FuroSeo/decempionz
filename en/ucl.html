History

From the European Cup to the Champions League

The European Cup was founded in 1955 by UEFA and journalist Gabriel Hanot, who proposed the competition after reading that English clubs were considered the best in Europe without ever having faced the continent's elite.

Real Madrid dominated the first five editions (1956–1960), with Di Stéfano and Puskas defining what a European dynasty looked like. Their record of five consecutive titles remains unmatched.

In 1992-93 UEFA rebranded the competition as the UEFA Champions League and introduced a group stage. That revolution changed the entire competition: more matches, many more clubs, and TV rights on a completely different scale. From 2024-25 the format expands further to 36 teams in a Swiss-system league phase.

Format

How the Champions League works

In the classic format (1992-2024): clubs qualified through domestic leagues play qualifying rounds, then a group stage of 8 groups of 4. The top two from each group advance to the Round of 16, then quarters, semis and a single-leg final.

From 2024-25: 36 clubs play a league phase (8 matches each, Swiss system). The top 8 advance directly to the Round of 16; positions 9-24 play a two-legged knockout. The bottom 12 are eliminated. From the Round of 16 onwards, it's pure knockout football.

Trivia

Did you know?

🥇

Real Madrid are the most successful club: 15 UCL/European Cup titles through 2024.

⚽

The highest-scoring UCL final remains Real Madrid vs. Eintracht Frankfurt 7-3 in 1960.

🔄

Liverpool came back from 0-3 at half-time against Milan in 2005 and won on penalties: the Istanbul final.

👑

Celtic 1966-67 were the first British club to win the European Cup, with a squad entirely born within 30 miles of Glasgow.
